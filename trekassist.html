TrekAssist

An intuitive and culturally adaptable interface for e-ink devices designed to guide visitors around Yellowstone National Park.

Role
UX Designer
Purpose
Computer Interface
Design Final
Team
3 product managers
2 designers
Tools
Figma, FigJam

Problem Statement

Yellowstone National Park receives over 4 million visitors annually, many of whom are international tourists unfamiliar with the park's layout, safety guidelines, and cultural significance. Current navigation tools are limited to paper maps and smartphone apps that drain battery life and require constant connectivity.

How might we...

...create a sustainable, accessible navigation solution that serves diverse visitors while respecting the park's natural environment?

Design Process

1

Ideation

Brainstormed solutions focused on e-ink technology for battery efficiency

2

Wireframing

Created low-fidelity prototypes testing different needs for the device

3

Refinement

Iterated based on feedback and accessibility standards

We prioritized a minimalist interface that works well on e-ink displays, with high contrast for outdoor visibility. The design supports 8 pages and has easily accessible safety features.

Key Learnings

Designing for outdoor environments requires careful consideration of readability, battery life, and diverse user needs. Cultural sensitivity and accessibility must be built into the foundation, not added as afterthoughts.
